FAQs

Dental implants are artificial tooth roots made of titanium, in the form of screws or roots, which are placed in the jawbone to replace the lost tooth and imitate tooth roots. The dental implant is biologically compatible with the body thanks to its titanium compounds.

In implant treatment that requires a surgical intervention, local anesthesia is applied to the person before the operation. For this reason, it is not possible for the person to feel pain or pain during the surgical intervention.

Tooth veneers types include porcelain, zirconium, laminate veneer, e-max coating.

The all on four implant technique (quad implant system) is a highly advanced form of conventional implants. In this method, instead of using one implant for each missing tooth, four upper and four lower implants are used as main anchors. Then the dental prosthesis is fixed on these implants.

You can return to work immediately after the operation.
